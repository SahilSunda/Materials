Designation : Graduate Natural Catastrophe Associate

Description :

About Swiss Re
Swiss Re is one of the world’s leading providers of reinsurance, insurance and other forms of insurance-based risk transfer, working to make the world more resilient. We anticipate and manage a wide variety of risks, from natural catastrophes and climate change to cybercrime.
At Swiss Re we combine experience with creative thinking and cutting-edge expertise to create new opportunities and solutions for our clients. This is possible thanks to the collaboration of more than 13,000 employees across the world.
We offer a flexible working environment where curious and adaptable people thrive. Are you interested in joining us?

Job Description:
We are looking for people with Doctoral degree / postgraduate degree with 2-3 years of relevant industrial experience with strong academic record for our graduates@swissre programme. The programme offers you the chance to join the dynamic, fast-moving world of a highly specialised financial services organisation. Over an 18-month period, you'll receive on-the-job training in your role.

The Opportunity:
The catastrophe peril modelling team is responsible for Swiss Re’s global natural catastrophe perils risk assessment. This team develops cutting-edge probabilistic and proprietary modeling technology for earthquakes, windstorm and flood hazards, with a clear business mandate to grow Swiss Re’s competitive edge in close cooperation with Swiss Re’s underwriters and clients.

You will be embedded in a team of 45+ members, located in Zurich, New York, Munich and Bangalore which covers a wide range of Atmospheric science, engineering, statistics and finance disciplines. As a natural catastrophe associate you will get chance to work in projects oriented in one of the peril teams which focus on earthquake, wind or flood and in Nat Cat underwriting advisory to various desks globally in Swiss Re (mainly USA, Zurich, London, Munich, and Bangalore). You will be located in the Asia Hub in Bangalore. You get in-depth exposure to Swiss Re’s proprietary natural perils modelling platform and to Swiss Re’s natural catastrophe reinsurance business. Depending upon your inclination and skills, you can expand into other areas such as underwriting and innovative insurance solutions after successful completion of your training.

As part of your 18-months training you will provide underwriting support to various desks and will work with perils teams to support next generation of our model platform. In parallel your training will focus on acquiring an in-depth understanding of a reinsurance business model and products, with an immediate application to the daily support of our global product underwriting community.
About You
•    Successfully completed Doctoral degree/ postgraduate degree or equivalent in Atmospheric Science or Engineering discipline. People with postgraduate degree with around 2 years of relevant industry experience are also encouraged to apply.
•    The area of expertise, which we look for, are (i) Earthquake (ii) Flood (iii) Atmospheric perils
•    Programming experience in Matlab/Python/R and GIS skills is a major asset
•    Independent thinking and creative problem-solving skills
•    At ease with teamwork and communication in a global team.
•    Strong motivation to learn about re/insurance
•    Above all: scientific and economical curiosity, combined with strong analytical skills and ability to explain complex problems with clarity and effectiveness

Please note this is a global graduate program and you will get exposure to global training and roles in Zurich

Purpose
With the global graduates@swissre programme we
aim to attract diverse talents with up to 3 years of
work experience to build a strong talent pipeline.
Since 2008 more than 600 graduates with study
backgrounds from Actuarial Science, Economics,
Engineering, Finance, Management, Physics,
Statistics to History and Social Science went through
the global graduates@swissre programme and our
internally designed training. The programme provides
an excellent opportunity for graduates to discover
Swiss Re, and to build a strong internal network by
interacting with people from different cultures and
business areas.
After the successful completion of the programme, the
graduates continue to work in attractive roles such as
Data Engineer, Underwriter, Actuarial or Investment
Analyst, Cyber or Natural Hazard Specialist.
Programme Duration and Content
The 18-month programme starts around the
beginning of September each year and is comprised
of the following elements:  On-the-job training in the "home-base"
department  General and specialised off-the-job training
(classroom, virtual and digital formats)  Project work  Mentoring & networking  Exposure to other parts of Swiss Re through a
job rotation
